# Chapter 12: Home

Morning light in Elara’s solar was thinner than in the lower halls, but it carried a quieter quality. The cold remained, yet here it seemed less absolute. A low fire burned in the grate with steady heat.

Lucian sat with his mother at the small table near the window. Young Selene was already there, legs tucked beneath her on a cushioned stool, watching the steam rise from her cup.

Elara poured a warm drink for him and set it within reach.

“You slept well?” she asked.

“Yes, Mother.”

“Good.” She buttered a piece of dark bread and passed it to Selene, who took it with both hands. “The wind was strong last night. I could hear it along the upper terraces.”

“It woke me once,” Lucian said.

Elara gave a small nod. “It often does at this time of year.”

They ate for a while in comfortable quiet. Selene swung one foot slowly under the table. Elara watched her for a moment, then looked back to her son.

“There will be a family dinner this evening,” she said. “All of us together.”

Lucian looked up. “Everyone?”

“Yes. Your father, your grandmother, your uncle, Lyra… and the others.” She did not elaborate. “It is proper that we sit together from time to time.”

Selene spoke without looking up from her bread. “Will there be the sweet cakes?”

Elara’s mouth curved slightly. “If the kitchens have prepared them.”

“I hope they have,” Selene said.

Lucian almost smiled. He returned to his own food. The morning felt simple and contained. No training. No weight of instruction. Only the three of them and the steady fire.

---

The ordinary severity of Blackspire reasserted itself as the morning advanced.

Lucian left the solar and moved through the upper corridors without urgency. The warmth of the small fire and his mother’s voice stayed with him only as a residual impression, already thinning against the colder stone. The banners hung still. The light remained thin and colorless. Servants passed him with lowered eyes and the same careful distance as always.

Further on, the keep began to show quiet signs of the evening ahead.

In one of the wider passages near the formal wing, two stewards directed a pair of younger attendants carrying polished Cold Silver settings wrapped in dark cloth. Their steps were measured, their voices low. A third servant moved ahead of them to open a side door that led toward the dining chambers. None of them looked at Lucian directly, but the purpose in their movement was clear. The house was preparing.

He continued past without slowing. The residual softness of the morning breakfast sat at a distance now, held inside the larger order of the keep rather than opposing it. Both belonged. The private warmth and the formal gathering were simply different faces of the same place.

By the time the light in the high windows had shifted toward afternoon, the corridors near the family wing had grown quieter still. The preparations continued somewhere out of sight. Lucian returned to his own chambers to wait.

---

Later, in his chambers, Lyra helped him prepare for the evening.

She straightened the fall of his dark tunic with precise hands, then adjusted the silver clasp at his throat.

“Hold still,” she said.

He held still.

“The left shoulder sits higher. There.” She smoothed the fabric once more. “You look exact. Father will notice.”

“Does it matter?”

“It always matters,” Lyra replied. Her voice was calm. “Especially when the whole table is present.”

She stepped back to regard him, then gave a small, satisfied nod.

“Ready?”

“Yes.”

“Good. Lady Elara will come for you soon.”

---

Elara was waiting in the antechamber when Lucian arrived. She had changed into darker, more formal attire. When she saw him she looked him over once, then held out her hand.

“Come.”

He placed his hand in hers. Together they walked the final corridor toward the dining hall.

The chamber beyond the doors was severe and elegant. Dark wood, Cold Silver accents, tall chairs set with precise symmetry. Light from the wall fixtures left the higher reaches of the room in shadow.

Most of the family was already seated.

Vesper sat at the head of the table. Lady Orlena occupied the place of seniority to his right. Dain sat further down, upright and exact. Mira sat with Lyra beside her. Sable sat with the twins, Riven and Thorne, on either side of her.

Elara led Lucian to their seats. Only when he was properly settled did she release his hand and take her own place.

For a time the only sounds were the quiet movements of serving staff and the soft clink of utensils.

Then Orlena spoke.

“The northern houses have been restless,” she said, her voice measured. “Messages have been moving more frequently than usual.”

Vesper did not look up from his plate. “I am aware.”

“They offer no clear reason.”

“They rarely do at first,” Vesper replied. “We will watch. Nothing more is required yet.”

Dain gave a slight nod of agreement but said nothing.

Vesper’s gaze moved briefly along the table, then to Elara.

“Selene?”

“She is already sleeping,” Elara answered. “The cold tires her early these days.”

“It is the season,” Sable said quietly. “Mine find the same.”

Mira glanced toward Lyra, then to Lucian. “The children have been well?”

“They have,” Elara said.

Lyra looked across the table at Lucian. “You held the morning forms longer yesterday. Dain said as much.”

Lucian met her eyes. “Yes.”

“Good,” she said simply.

Vesper’s gaze rested on his son for a moment. “Continue as you have been. The foundation is not yet finished.”

“Yes, Father.”

Riven shifted slightly in his seat. Thorne remained still. Neither spoke. The conversation ebbed again into the quieter rhythm of the meal. Dishes were replaced. Wine was poured for the adults.

Lucian ate carefully and listened.

These were the people who formed the inner circle of the house. His father at the head. His mother beside him. His older half-sister Lyra beside Mira. His grandmother. His uncle. Mira and Sable, present and composed. Riven and Thorne beside their mother. The table held them all.

He did not feel the need to speak further. The morning’s private warmth with Elara and Selene still existed behind the formal atmosphere, just as the lessons of the forges and the Veil and the high roosts still existed. All of it belonged here. All of it was home.

Outside, the wind moved across the high stone of Blackspire. Inside, the family table held its silence and its order.

He remained where he was, among his own.
